Alligator

  • Let's Learn

An alligator is a large reptile with short legs. While crocodiles have a shorter and rounder
snout, alligators have a pointier snout. The two extant species are the American alligator and
the Chinese alligator. Several extinct species of alligator are known from fossil remains.
Reptiles are cold-blooded animals that lay eggs and are covered in scales or hard plates.
Alligators are no reptile runts. They can grow up to 15 feet long and weigh about 1,000 pounds.
A gator's coat is rough and scaled. The young alligators are black with bright yellow blotches and
stripes, and the adults are a uniform dark color.
