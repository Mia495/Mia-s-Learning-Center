Llama

  • Let's Learn

Llamas are related to camels, and like camels, are used to carry
goods over long distances. A llama can carry as much as 200
pounds for 12 hours a day, but are not ridden. The llama's high
thirst tolerance, endurance, and ability to subsist on a wide
variety of forage makes it an important transport animal on
the bleak Andean plateaus and mountains. The llama is a gentle
animal, but, when overloaded or maltreated, it will lie down,
hiss, spit and kick, and refuse to move.
