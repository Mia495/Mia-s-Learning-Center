Lobster

  • Let's Learn

Lobsters are animals that live in all of the world's oceans. They are
invertebrates, which means they have no backbone, but they do have
an exoskeleton to help protect their inside body parts. Lobsters have
two big claws and eight walking legs. One claw is the crusher claw,
which helps the lobster destroy something he wants to eat. The other
claw is called the pincher or ripper claw and is used to tear food apart.
They eat both plants and animals. Many people eat lobster for food.
