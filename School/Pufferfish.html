Pufferfish

  • Let's Learn

A pufferfish is a fish that is usually poisonous, normally lives in warm ocean waters
and can blow up into a ball several times its regular size, and it is deadly to humans
and some animals if eaten. They take in oxygen through special organs called gills, so
they can breathe even when they are puffed up. Pufferfish have tough, usually prickly
skins. The spiny puffer has long spines all over its body. When it inflates, the spines
stick out like porcupine quills.
