Rhinoceros

  • Let's Learn

Rhinoceroses are large, heavy animals with thick gray or brown
skin, short legs, and one or two horns on their nose. They
are herbivores and only eat plants. Rhinoceroses are endangered,
meaning there are very few left, because they have been hunted
for the horns on their nose. Rhinoceroses are universally recognized
by their massive bodies, stumpy legs and either one or two dermal
horns. In some species, the horns may be short or not obvious.
They are renowned for having poor eyesight, but their senses of
smell and hearing are well developed.
