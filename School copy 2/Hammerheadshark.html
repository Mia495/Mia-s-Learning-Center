Hammerhead Shark

  • Let's Learn

Hammerhead sharks have a stout body and classically shark-shaped with a
markedly tall, curved, first dorsal fin. The great hammerhead is dusky
brown to light gray on the dorsal surface, fading to a cream-colored
underside, with an asymmetrical caudal fin. They can reach a maximum
length of 18 to 20 feet. Hammerhead sharks do not lay eggs like most
other fish. Instead, they give birth to live young. Their babies are called
pups. Young hammerhead sharks can swim as soon as they are born.
Hammerhead sharks are carnivores, which means they only eat meat. They
feed on stingrays but can also eat fish, crabs, squid, and lobsters.
