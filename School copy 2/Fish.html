Fish

  • Let's Learn

Fish are aquatic vertebrate animals that have gills but lack limbs with
digits, like fingers or toes. Recall that vertebrates are animals with
internal backbones. Most fish are streamlined in their general body form.
Unlike mammals, fish are cold-blooded. This means that they do not maintain
a constant internal body temperature; instead, their temperature is greatly
influenced by their environment. True fish have a backbone and fins. Most also
breathe with gills and have scales that cover their bodies.
