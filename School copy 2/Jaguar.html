Jaguar

  • Let's Learn

The jaguar looks much like the leopard. But the jaguar is larger and has
bigger black spots. The average jaguar is between 5 and 6 feet long,
not including the tail. Males weigh between 220 and 350 pounds. Some
jaguars are so dark they don't seem to have spots. Unlike many other
cats, jaguars do not avoid water; in fact, they are quite good swimmers.
They are creative hunters that can hunt their prey in various ways.
They can pounce on them in the treetops or even in the water. In contrast
to most cats, they are not afraid of the water and fare quite well.
