Eel

  • Let's Learn

Even though the eel looks like a snake, it is actually a fish. They
have long, narrow bodies with long dorsal and anal fins. Most eels
have no scales. The eel's backbone is made up of over 100 vertebrae
which makes it very flexible. Eels are characterized by their elongated,
wormlike bodies. Unlike most fish, eels do not have pelvic fins, and most
species do not have pectoral fins. Eels have a continuous dorsal, anal,
and caudal fin that runs around the tail tip. Eels generate waves that
travel the length of their bodies to help them swim.
