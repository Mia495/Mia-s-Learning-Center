Goldfish

  • Let's Learn

Goldfish are a golden-yellow or orange fish that live in fish tanks or
human-made ponds as pets. They are freshwater fish, meaning they live
in water that isn't salty. The reason their eyes look bulgy is because they
don't have eyelids and can't blink, but they have great eyesight and can see
up to 16 feet away. Goldfish hatch with dark, pigmented scales, but they
depigment 2 to 3 months after hatching. Adult goldfish are a yellow-orange
color and possess transparent fins. They can come in colors like red, black,
and silver due to a variety of factors including diet, light, and illness.
