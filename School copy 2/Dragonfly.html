Dragonfly

  • Let's Learn

Dragonflies are a type of flying insect. They are recognised by their distinct
appearence of a long body with two pairs of strong, transparent wings. They
vary in colour, including red, brown, blue and yellow. Many dragonflies have
brilliant iridescent or metallic colours produced by structural colouration,
making them conspicuous in flight. They are some of the fastest insects on
earth and can reach speeds of almost 50 kilometers per hour when in flight.
Their muscles are attached directly to their wings which allows them to fly
faster than other insects. This also helps them be one of the most accurate
hunters as they can fly forward, backward, and upside-down. They can also
hover and pivot in place.
