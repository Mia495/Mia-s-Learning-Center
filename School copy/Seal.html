Seal

  • Let's Learn

A seal's body is round in the middle and narrower at the ends.
The skin is usually brown, gray, or black. The smallest seals
are about 4 feet long and weigh about 200 pounds. The largest
seals can be 21 feet long and weigh more than 8,000 pounds.
Seals are semi-aquatic mammals that are in a group called pinnipeds,
which means fin-footed. There are 32 species of web-footed aquatic
mammals that live chiefly in cold seas and whose body shape is round
at the middle and tapered at the ends. It is adapted for swift and
graceful swimming.
